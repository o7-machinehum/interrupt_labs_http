It starts as a boring flash drive.

Plug Phantomdrive in, and the host sees a regular USB mass storage device. The first section behaves like normal storage. The rest stays hidden until the unlock pattern is written.

Phantomdrive Unlock Flow

  1. 01

    Mounts normally

    Phantomdrive first appears as a normal USB drive.

  2. 02

    Waits for the trigger

    Create a file called unlock.txt containing password:addyourpasswordhere.

  3. 03

    Intercepts the write

    The firmware prevents that password file from persisting on storage.

  4. 04

    Reveals the rest

    The remaining disk is remounted, and data is encrypted and decrypted in place.

Project Details

  • Open hardware:Hardware, firmware, software, and mechanical files are open source.
  • Easy updates:The CH569W firmware can be updated by pressing the reset button while inserting it into your PC. No programmer required.
  • KDF:The key derivation function is open.
  • Measured speeds:We've recorded 20 MB/s reads and 9 MB/s writes.
  • Designed in KiCad:You do not need to pay thousands to edit the design files.
  • UART:UART is available through TP1, TP2, and TP3.
  • Nondescript enclosure:The enclosure has no special markings.
  • SD card storage:An SD card is used for storage, so you determine the capacity.

FAQ

Questions and Answers

When will my device ship?

The campaign will run until July 15th, 2026. I will start manufacturing after, which will take 4-8 weeks. The first backers should start seeing their Phantomdrives after that.

Why Crowdfunding?

I want to aggregate initial orders so everyone can get a device. My usual customers will tell you I'm usually sold out, this is because as a very small maker I can't take the risk to order large initial volumes.

Why not Kickstarter?

I have used crowdfunding platforms before; they take a cut, which increases the price for you.

Do you has a history of shipping hardware?

Yes! Ovrdrive was very successful. I've also shipped hundreds of Flipper Blackhats and Blankpants.

How far along is the project?

The engineering is finished, and the devices have been tested. There are 30 units out in the field already.

Isn't this just VeraCrypt?

No. A normal encrypted volume exposes random-looking encrypted data. Phantomdrive presents normal enencrypted data, then reveals the protected section only after the unlock trigger.

Is entering a password into a text file insecure?

The firmware snoops for unlock write and will inhibit it.

Why use an SD card?

Because AI fucked up the cost of eMMC.

Can't you just crack open the device and take out the SD card?

Yes, but all the data is encrypted.

What KDF do you use?

AES(sha256(password | padding | salt)) 100k sha256's. The salt is "per device". If you want a different KDF, DIY :)
